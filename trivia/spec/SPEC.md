# Moku Room — Concept

> Status: idea / concept. Target repo: `moku-labs/claude`.
> The technical side (transport, hosting, state) is handled by **Moku**. This doc is about the idea.

## The idea

A way to make **couch multiplayer party games** for the browser, where one big screen
is the shared display and everyone's phone is a controller.

The reference point is the kind of experience Sony did with party games like
*Knowledge is Power*: you start a game on the TV, your phone becomes the controller,
and a group plays together in the same room.

The experience:

1. Open a link in the **TV browser**. It shows the game and a **QR code**.
2. Each player **scans the QR with their phone** and joins the room.
3. The **TV is the shared stage** — everyone looks at it together.
4. The **phones are private controllers** — each player picks and taps on their own screen.

Two things to build:

- **Moku Room** — the foundation for making this kind of game (the shared-screen + phones pattern).
- **Trivia** — the first game built on it, to prove the concept end to end.

---

## Trivia — the first game

A party quiz for **1–5 players**, **12 rounds** per match.

- At the start, the group **picks a language** (English by default, Russian always available).
- Each round, the active player **chooses a category**, then answers a question from it.
- Every question has **4 options**, and can be **text** or an **image**.
- **If the active player answers wrong**, the chance to answer **passes to the next player** (a steal).
- **Correct answers earn points.**
- **Difficulty rises gradually** through the match — it starts easy and gets harder by the final rounds.

The feel we want: fast, social, looks great on the big screen, easy to tap on a phone.

---

## The questions

Questions and categories are **generated by a Claude skill** that ships with the game.

What the skill should do:

- **Generate** fresh categories and questions on demand.
- Work in **multiple languages** — English first, **Russian required**.
- Handle **text and image** questions (images pulled from the internet).
- **Check quality** with review agents before a question is accepted: facts correct, one clear right answer, believable wrong options, fits its difficulty, reads well.
- **Grade difficulty** so a match can ramp from easy to hard.
- **Hide the answers** in the saved files, so the author can't accidentally spoil themselves while looking at them.

And during play:

- **Never repeat a question** the group has already seen — each player's history is remembered on their own phone, so old questions don't come back in future matches.

## Open questions about the idea

- Should scores carry over between matches, or reset each game?
- What happens when a chosen category runs out of fresh questions?
- How many categories does a player choose from each round?

## Sample questions (to show the shape)

A few examples across languages, types, and difficulty — just to picture the content.

- **Easy / EN / text** — "Which planet is known as the Red Planet?" → Mars, Venus, Jupiter, Mercury.
- **Easy / RU / text** — "Сколько цветов традиционно выделяют в радуге?" → 5, 6, 7, 8.
- **Medium / EN / image** — a flag shown on screen: "Which country does this flag belong to?" → Japan, Bangladesh, Palau, South Korea.
- **Medium / RU / text** — "Химическая формула воды?" → CO2, H2O, O2, NaCl.
- **Hard / RU / text** — "В каком году человек впервые полетел в космос?" → 1957, 1961, 1969, 1965.
- **Hard / EN / image** — a painting shown on screen: "Who painted this work?" → Van Gogh, Monet, Cézanne, Munch.
